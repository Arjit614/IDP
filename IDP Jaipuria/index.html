C.N.S.A.
(Chinese National Space Administration)

CNSA is an agency created in 1993 to develop science and technology in China. China has signed governmental space cooperation agreements with Brazil, Chile, France, Germany, India, Italy, Pakistan, Russia, Ukraine, UK and US. The CNSA has it's headquarters in Haidan District, Beijing. It's administrator now is Zhang Kegian. It is the 2nd best Space agency in the world with a budget of $11 Billion. Since 2003 they have conducted many manned space missions and so far, 11 chinese astronauts have travelled in space. China has improved so much from 1993. Some of its missions are- Tianwen-1, Asteroid Exploration Mission(ZhengHe), Mars Sample Return Mission etc.

In contrast to most other space agencies it is not involved with the ISS(International Space Station) and in fact has it's own small space station.Since 2003, when Yang Liwei became the first Chinese national in space, CNSA has done several manned space launches. In 2013, a three-person crew aboard Shenzhou 9 made the first Chinese manned docking in space, attaching to the single-room station, Tiangong 1. The agency succeeded in making the first soft landing on the moon in decades in December 2014 with its Chang'e 3 lander and its rover, Yutu. About it's logo

CNSA's logo is a similar design to that of China Aerospace Science and Technology Corporation. The arrow in the middle is similar to the Chinese character 人 which means 'human' or 'people', to state that humans are the center of all space exploration. The three concentric ellipses stand for three types of Escape Velocity (minimum speed needed to reach sustainable orbits, to escape the earth system, and to escape the solar system) which are milestones of space exploration. The second ring is drawn with a bold line, to state that China has passed the first stage of exploration (earth system) and is undergoing the second stage exploration (within the solar system). The 人 character stands above the three rings to emphasize humanity's capability to escape and explore. Olive branches were added to state that China's space exploration is peaceful in nature.
